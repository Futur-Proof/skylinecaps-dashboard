Business Model

Five Revenue Streams

Every lead that enters the platform monetizes across multiple paths. Nothing is wasted.

Stream 01

Lead Origination & Resale

Inbound platform captures SMB applicants via SEO and YouTube. Unworked leads are packaged and sold to brokers and lenders.

$0.50–$200 per lead
Stream 02

Brokerage (Commission)

Qualified leads are placed with lender partners. No capital required. Pure commission on every funded deal.

8–12% of funded amount
Stream 03

Direct Lending

Best deals are funded directly using investor capital. Higher margin than brokering. Daily/weekly ACH repayments.

30–50% return / 6–8 mo
Stream 04

Investor Capital Returns

Accredited investors deploy $50K–$500K+. Platform allocates to funded loans, credits returns as repayments come in.

20–25% target investor return
Stream 05

Data Marketplace

Structured marketplace for tiered lead sales. Aged, fresh, and real-time exclusive listings sold to verified buyers.

Resell same data 3–5x
The Flywheel
Lead comes in → Qualify & Tier → Gold gets funded directly → Investors earn 20–25% → Silver gets brokered → $8K–$25K commission → Bronze gets listed on marketplace → $0.50–$45/lead sold 3–5x → Revenue from every tier covers cost of lead acquisition → Net lead cost approaches $0

Third-Party Stack

Integrations

Every integration has a specific role. No overlap, no redundancy.

Plaid

  • Borrower bank connection during application (Link Token → Public Token exchange)
  • Transaction sync — identify deposits, compute avg monthly deposits
  • Identity verification — legal name match
  • Investor ACH setup via processor token → Stripe bank account
  • Access tokens encrypted via Supabase Vault

Stripe

  • Investor deposits — PaymentElement, ACH debit for large amounts
  • Marketplace purchases — charge buyer card on file
  • Loan repayments — scheduled ACH debits, daily queue
  • Commission payouts — Stripe Connect or manual ACH
  • Stripe Customer objects for investors and data buyers

Resend

  • Lead confirmation to borrower
  • Application status updates
  • Document request emails
  • Loan offer and funded confirmations
  • Daily repayment confirmations
  • Monthly investor statements (PDF attached)
  • Marketplace purchase receipts

Supabase

  • Postgres — primary data store, all tables
  • Auth — email/password + magic link + MFA (TOTP)
  • RLS — row-level security on every table
  • Vault — Plaid token encryption (pgsodium)
  • Storage — documents, statements, accreditation docs
  • Realtime — leads, repayments, investor returns, notifications
  • Edge Functions — async work (Plaid sync, PDF gen, webhooks)
  • pg_cron — lead aging, statements, repayment queue

Middesk / Persona

  • KYB (Know Your Business) — EIN verification
  • OFAC sanctions screening
  • Beneficial ownership verification
  • Required for direct lending arm compliance

Parallel Markets (Phase 2)

  • Investor accreditation verification service
  • Third-party verification letter from CPA/attorney
  • Required for Reg D 506(c) compliance
  • Replaces manual document review

Boberdoo / LeadsPedia

  • Ping-post lead routing — push a lead, lenders bid or accept/reject in real time with a price returned instantly
  • Immediate access to lender networks with zero individual outreach — lenders already on the network
  • Boberdoo: most widely used for MCA and SMB lenders; LeadsPedia: lending-native, strong credit union and specialty lender coverage
  • Set up criteria once; routing runs automatically on every submission
  • Day-one lender access before any ISO agreements are signed

Lendflow

  • Real-time multi-lender decisioning API — push borrower data, receive multiple offers with rates and terms in one call
  • Powers the "here are your best offers before you apply" UI on /apply/offers
  • Each lender's pre-loaded criteria matched automatically; no manual submission per lender
  • Returns APR-equivalent, factor rate, and term side-by-side for borrower comparison
  • Phase 2 integration — replaces manual offer-building step in the rep workflow

Direct ISO Partner APIs

  • OnDeck, Bluevine, Fundbox, Lendistry, LendingFront — all maintain ISO/broker partner programs with submission APIs
  • Sign broker agreement → receive API credentials → push lead package, receive decision and offer programmatically
  • Higher commissions than ping-post; direct relationship and dedicated underwriting contacts
  • Same lenders named by Plaid as primary SMB lending customers
  • Target: 3 to 5 signed ISO agreements by end of Month 2
Lender Infrastructure

Lender Routing Sequence

The lender market is fragmented by design. No single API connects all of them. This is the realistic build sequence — ordered by speed to first placement.

Timeline
Action
Now
Sign up for Boberdoo or LeadsPedia. Lenders already on the network are immediately reachable. Zero individual outreach required. Set your criteria once — leads route and price automatically.
Month 1–2
Direct ISO agreements with OnDeck, Bluevine, Lendistry, LendingFront, Fundbox. All have published partner programs. Sign the broker agreement, get API credentials, push deals programmatically. Higher commissions and dedicated contacts versus ping-post.
Month 2–3
Integrate Lendflow for real-time multi-lender decisioning. Push borrower profile, receive multiple lender offers with rates and terms in one call. Powers the "here are your best options" UI before the borrower formally applies — highest-converting feature in the flow.
Month 3+
Banks and credit unions via state leagues and CUSOs. Traditional CUs run on legacy LOS systems (Jack Henry, Fiserv, FIS) with no public lead intake APIs. Reach business lending officers directly, contact state credit union leagues for referral programs, and evaluate Blend or nCino integrations as a longer-term play to reach multiple institutions at once.

Middesk — KYB Only

Middesk's role is KYB verification: EIN, OFAC, beneficial ownership. It is not a lender matching tool. For lender matching and offer surfacing, use Lendflow. These are distinct tools with distinct roles — Middesk gates compliance; Lendflow gates lender routing.

Nav — Matched Offers Marketplace

Nav is a business credit and lending marketplace with an API. Push a borrower profile, receive back matched offers from lenders who have pre-loaded criteria. Secondary option to Lendflow for offer surfacing — evaluate both in Month 2–3 and run the one with better lender coverage for the target verticals.

Legal & Compliance

Regulatory Framework

Three compliance domains: alternative lending, investor securities, and data privacy.

⚖

Regulation D (Investor Side)

506(b) allows up to 35 non-accredited sophisticated investors, no general solicitation. 506(c) allows general solicitation but requires verified accreditation. Form D filed with SEC within 15 days of first investor sale.

📋

MCA Disclosure (CA, NY, VT, VA, UT)

MCA is a purchase of future receivables, not a loan — but CA SB 1235 and NY Commercial Finance Disclosure Law require APR-equivalent disclosure for CA and NY applicants. Computed and displayed at /apply/review.

🏦

KYB / AML / BSA

EIN verification, OFAC screening, beneficial ownership checks via Middesk or Persona. Required before any direct loan funding. Results stored on businesses table.

🔐

ACH Authorization

All borrowers must sign ACH debit authorization before repayments begin. Signed mandate logged with timestamp on funded_loans table. Never initiate debit without signed mandate.

🛡

Data Encryption

Plaid access tokens encrypted via Supabase Vault (pgsodium). SSN stored as last-4 only unless full storage required (then Vault-encrypted). AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.2+ in transit.

👤

PII Handling

Borrower PII never logged in lead_events or crm_activities. Marketplace lead_data_snapshot scrubbed of Plaid tokens, SSN, and internal routing data. Stale leads anonymized after 3 years via pg_cron.

🔒

Auth & MFA

MFA enforced for admin and investor roles. Two-layer enforcement: Next.js middleware (route-level) + Supabase RLS (query-level). Service-role key never in client bundles.

📊

Rate Limiting & API Security

Public /api/leads limited to 10 req/min per IP via Vercel Edge Middleware. External buyer API: 100 req/min per API key. Stripe and Plaid webhooks verified via signing secrets.
