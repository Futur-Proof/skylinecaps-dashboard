The Arbitrage

Buy aged leads at $1 to $3. Work the best as broker deals and earn $10K commission. Fund the cream directly for 30 to 50% returns. Resell leftovers at $1 to $3. The resale covers acquisition cost. Everything else is margin.

The Moat

ISO shops buy data. LendingTree sells data. Nobody does both plus direct lending. The more data Skyline originates, the better the marketplace becomes. The better the lending book, the more investors join. Compounding moats at every layer.

The Durability

When Chase tightens, B-paper expands. When PayPal declines 100K leads, those leads need somewhere to go. Every time big banks get more restrictive, Skyline's addressable market grows larger.

Industry Targeting

Best Verticals to Own

These industries have recurring capital needs, high average monthly deposits, and strong urgency around speed. They get turned down by banks for reasons that have nothing to do with their cash flow.

Construction
Gets paid in 30 days, paid in 60. Always has a new job to bid on. Cash-rich but timing-poor. Has $300K sitting in the account but would rather deploy someone else's capital to keep their own dry.
Gold tier typical High avg deposits Seasonal spikes
Trucking
Fuel costs, maintenance, driver pay. Cash flow gaps between loads and receivables. Fleet expansion opportunities move fast. Banks dislike the asset-light model and reject viable operators constantly.
High frequency Silver to Gold High renewal rate
Restaurants
High daily card volume makes Plaid verification fast and clean. Seasonal swings mean capital needs are predictable. Banks reject restaurant risk profiles. MCA was practically invented for this vertical.
Easy to underwrite Silver typical High renewal rate
Healthcare
Insurance reimbursement delays destroy cash flow for practices with real revenue. Dentists, med spas, urgent care centers are all profitable but have AR timing issues. High average loan size and low default rates.
Gold tier typical Educated borrowers Low default rate
Landscaping and Home Services
Equipment purchases, crew expansion, seasonal front-loading. Strong recurring revenue but lumpy by design. Owners are operators first, finance people never. They want fast, simple, and done.
Silver typical Seasonal demand High volume
Retail and Supermarkets
Inventory timing, seasonal stock-up, expansion opportunities. High daily transaction volume makes Plaid underwriting straightforward. Grocery stores especially — massive deposits, tight margins, frequent capital needs year-round.
Fast Plaid verify High deposits Repeat borrowers
Revenue Model

How Each Dollar Gets Made

Five revenue streams from a single lead. The first two are live from day one. The last three scale as volume grows.

Lead Resale — Data Marketplace
$0.50 to $200 per lead
Bronze leads auto-listed. Fresh leads listed at 7 days. Fully qualified unused leads listed as real-time exclusive. Same lead sold up to five times to different buyers.
Brokerage Commission
8 to 12% of funded amount
Silver and Gold leads placed with lender partners. No capital deployed. Pure commission. A $100K funded deal produces $8K to $12K in the door. Ten deals per month equals $80K to $120K gross.
Direct Lending Return
30 to 50% over 6 to 8 months
Best Gold leads funded directly. $250K funded at 1.35 factor rate equals $337,500 total payback. Minus investor capital cost of 20 to 25%, Skyline keeps the spread as earned income.
Investor Platform Fee
2% of returns credited
On every repayment credited to investors, Skyline deducts a 2% platform management fee before passing through the net return.
Renewal Commission
4 to 6% on renewals
When a funded borrower returns for a second loan — common in MCA at a 60 to 70% renewal rate — the commission is earned again with zero acquisition cost on that deal.

Execution Timeline
Week 1 to 4
Cold Outreach
Start with what already works. Cold email and cold call using existing business data. Target UCC filers in the top three verticals: construction, trucking, restaurants. You have the list, the scripts, and the closers. This funds the platform build while development runs in parallel.
Month 1 to 2
Declined Data Buyback
Reach out to 3 to 5 direct lenders for their declined pipeline. OnDeck, Kabbage (AmEx), Bluevine all have leads they cannot place. Buy their monthly batch at low cost. You know the product and can work it from day one. Instant volume with high data quality.
Month 2 to 3
Referral Network
Sign five accountants or bookkeepers on referral commission. Offer 1 to 2% of the funded amount. One good CPA firm sends 3 to 4 Gold leads a month. At $250K average that is $7,500 to $10,000 commission per referral partner per month. Pays for itself on the first deal closed.
Month 3 to 4
YouTube Ads
Educational content targeting business owners by industry. Videos like "How to get $250K without showing tax returns" run in front of construction, restaurant, and trucking content. CPM is low. Hook on the problem, land on the application form. Attribution tracked from view to funded deal via UTM and Plaid connect completion events.
Month 4 to 6
Google Paid Search
Start narrow on highest-intent, lowest-CPC keywords. Industry-specific terms like "construction company working capital" before touching broad "business loan" terms. Confirm which keywords produce funded deals before scaling spend. Attribution first, budget second.
Month 6 Plus
SEO Content Engine
Build topical authority in the SMB lending space. One deep page per industry vertical, one page per keyword cluster. Six to twelve months to rank but compounding free traffic that grows forever. This is how you reduce paid acquisition cost to near zero over time.
